To help out with link blogging I created a few skills (technically they are /commands).

- /link-new <url> to create a new post file with frontmatter pre-populated ([link](https://github.com/sted9000/claude-skills/blob/main/.claude/commands/link-new.md))

- /link-review to proof your post to make sure it is up to your standards ([link](https://github.com/sted9000/claude-skills/blob/main/.claude/commands/link-review.md))

- /link-publish to publish your post ([link](https://github.com/sted9000/claude-skills/blob/main/.claude/commands/link-publish.md))

Bonus

- /photo to take a photo from your clipboard and host it for you to easily insert in your post ([link](https://github.com/sted9000/claude-skills/blob/main/.claude/commands/photo.md))

"Automations" like these are cool because they break the stereotype that automations hinder creativity, thinking and collaboration -- these ones push me to think more, be original, and share!

I was inspired by [My approach to running a link blog](https://simonwillison.net/2024/Dec/22/link-blog/).
